# RESEARCH-BASED-CANCER-DETECTION-WITH-EXPLAINABLE-AI

In this project, I bridge the gap between academic research and real-world
Medical AI. I move away from using pre-packaged, beginner-friendly models
and instead take on the challenge of a Senior AI Researcher: implementing a novel,
state-of-the-art architecture from a research paper.

I build ThyroCheck AI, a diagnostic tool that classifies thyroid nodules from
complex ultrasound images. To achieve high precision, I code a custom deep
learning architecture known as FibonacciNet from scratch.

Crucially, because medical professionals cannot trust a "black box" algorithm, I
integrate Explainable AI (XAI). I implement Grad-CAM to generate visual
heatmaps, proving exactly why the model made its diagnosis. Finally, I package
this intelligence into an automated pipeline that generates comprehensive, clinician-
ready diagnostic reports.

# What i Learn?

--> Advanced Deep Learning: Master the skill of building custom network architectures (PyTorch or
TensorFlow) rather than just calling pre-trained models.
--> Explainable AI (XAI): Learn to implement Grad-CAM, a highly sought-after skill for ensuring AI
fairness, accountability, and transparency.
--> Medical Computer Vision: Gain expertise in processing and augmenting noisy medical
ultrasound datasets.
--> Research Literacy: Develop the ability to read state-of-the-art AI research papers and
implement their findings in code.
--> Clinical MLOps: Understand how to format AI outputs into automated, consumable reports for
non-technical end-users.

# Why this Project?

Research Implementation: The ability to implement a custom architecture
(FibonacciNet) from a paper proves you are an advanced engineer, not just a
script-kiddie.

The "XAI" Factor: "Explainable AI" is one of the most critical buzzwords in
enterprise AI today. Showing you know how to build interpretable models makes
your portfolio stand out to rigorous industries (Finance, Healthcare, Law).

Clinical Readiness: Generating an automated PDF report shows product-minded
thinking—you understand that the AI is useless unless its output is easily
consumable by a doctor.
